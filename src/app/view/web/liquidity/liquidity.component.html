What Is a Clearing Account

Once a new Forex or CFD firm is established, it will need a Clearing Account.

A Clearing Account is an account that allows FX or CFD firm to hedge its clients’ trades and mitigate a market risk.

Each successful and reputable FX or CFD firm will have a Clearing Account to manage adverse market conditions and clients’ trades.

eForex provides a Corporate Clearing Accounts with narrow spreads to FX and CFD firms.

A Firm can have a Clearing Account as an outright Corporate Account or as a Corporate API Account.

What Is a Liquidity Account

Each FX or CFD firm, as the business grows, will need one or two Liquidity Accounts with different Counterparties to spread and mitigate its market risk.

A Liquidity Account is an account that allows FX or CFD firm to hedge its clients’ trades and mitigate a market risk among different Trading Counterparties.

Each successful and reputable FX or CFD firm will have a Clearing Account and one or two additional Liquidity Accounts to manage adverse market conditions and clients’ trades when there is a problem with one or the other Counterparty.

eForex provides a variety of Liquidity Accounts with narrow spreads and different margins to FX and CFD firms.

A Firm can have a Liquidity Account as an outright Corporate Account or as a Corporate API Account.
